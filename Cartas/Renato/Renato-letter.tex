	\textbf{Letter of recommendation for Renato Andrade}
	
	
	To whom it may concern, 
	
	I am writing to recommend the candidate \textbf{Renato Andrade} in \textbf{very high terms} for a PhD position at IMPA.  \\ 
	
	I have known Renato, and have been his master's thesis advisor, for around a year now.  In our first interaction, it was already clear to me that Renato had a deep interest for mathematical analysis: indeed, he did his undergrad under the guidance of Dami\~ao Ara\'ujo at UFPB, where he developed a taste for analysis early on.  Within minutes of getting to know me, he already asked for a topic in which he could work in parallel to the courses at IMPA.
	
	I believe this to be one of Renato's strongest suits: he is {\bf passionate about research in analysis}, to the point that he is {\bf always determined to learn more.} As such, I have only had {\bf exciting interactions} with Renato so far. We have been meeting about once every two weeks for the past year to discuss mathematics, and he has always asked the right question about the problems he works on.
	
	Indeed,  he came to me a couple of months ago with the very simple question of explaining intrinsically Duhamel's formula.  After I explained to him its proof, he told me he had already managed to prove it - his issue was with the motivation behind it.  As it turns out, this was a question I had never encountered before, especially posed by a master's student. The ensuing discussion produced not only a satisfactory answer to his question, but also showed me a new way to think about the topic at hand. 
	
	Examples of such instances do not stop there. He was one of the most participative students in my course ``PDE: Linear theory'', and he asked, on several occasions, fundamental questions about generalizations to theorems seen in class.  In more than one of such instances, his question directly led to topics which would later be seen in class, which showcases a deep understanding of the usefulness of the tools discussed.  Furthermore, he did very well in the course itself, where he {\bf obtained a final grade of A-}, all while trying to solve and submit all the proposed exercises in the assignments, regardless of their difficulty. This grade is, in my view, all the more impressive when one takes into account that the cohort of students taking the course alongside Renato was, by some margin, {\bf one of the strongest cohorts I have ever taught} -- the course itself being a notoriously tough one --, so that performing at this level was far from straightforward.

In a similar spirit, Renato also took my topics course on `Geometric Inequalities', where he obtained a final grade of B+. While, at first sight, this grade is somewhat below what I believe Renato is capable of, I would like to emphasize that he had some {\bf bad luck on the first exam}, an issue which he largely overcame in the second exam, where his {\bf performance was very good} indeed. Throughout the course, Renato was, once more, an engaged and curious presence, regularly raising insightful questions and visibly eager to push the material beyond what was being covered in class.
	
	Speaking of grades, I {\bf strongly believe} Renato's grades, taken as a whole, do not fully depict how competent he actually is, and that he, in fact, {\bf consistently underperforms with respect to his actual competence level}.  Indeed, Renato is so deeply interested in mathematical analysis that it seems to be the case that he has mostly worked on learning topics besides the ones from the courses he takes at IMPA, mainly because he is more interested in the other (more analytic) ones.  While I do believe that this is one of the points where Renato can improve the most, by simply better allocating and balancing his time and effort,  I believe there is a `glass-half-full' way to look at this: he has shown that he is capable of keeping reasonable grades (which by now float around the A-/B+ range) in spite of starting out with a tough semester at IMPA, while, at the same time, learning more about his topic of interest.  This makes me excited about what he might be able to achieve if given the freedom to focus mostly on analysis.
	 
	Renato's master's thesis is on the paper `A remark on harmonic gradients in dimensions $\geq 3$', by J. Bourgain and T. Wolff, on which we have been working together for about 5 months now. This is, by all accounts, an {\bf extremely tough paper in modern harmonic analysis}, and I had originally estimated that it would take Renato around 8 months simply to digest its main ideas. To my pleasant surprise, he is, at this stage, {\bf already done with understanding the bulk of the paper}, and has done so {\bf considerably faster than I had anticipated}, to the point that we are now in a position to push beyond its content into a first original research project -- something which, given his admission to IMPA's PhD program, should naturally progress to a first research topic in his PhD. The depth and speed with which he absorbed this material reinforced my belief that Renato has {\bf enormous potential as a researcher}, and that he should be given the opportunity to develop it within IMPA's PhD program.

I should also mention that, alongside his master's work, Renato has been an {\bf excellent teaching assistant} for my course `Introduction to Differential Equations'. He has been in charge of {\bf coordinating the exercise classes}, where he {\bf proposed many of the exercises himself} and frequently suggested improvements to the way the material was presented and to the structure of the assignments. In this capacity, he has become an {\bf invaluable member of my teaching team}, and I have come to rely on his initiative, judgment, and mathematical taste to a degree that is rather uncommon for a student at his stage.

Taking all of the above into consideration, I would place Renato comfortably within the {\bf top 10--15\%} of all the students with whom I have ever interacted, and I have no doubt that, given the opportunity, he has the potential to become a {\bf strong researcher in mathematical analysis}.
	
	Finally, another quality one cannot ignore about Renato are his {\bf social skills}. Since the first day we interacted, Renato has always been respectful, while also being easy-going and talkative. It is therefore clear to me that he enjoys the collaborative aspect of mathematics,  since he effectively engages in discussions about the problems in the assignments from the `PDE: Linear Theory' class with colleagues, while also keeping an independent mind whenever it comes to writing up the problems themselves. 
	
	These qualities make me \textbf{exceptionally excited} at the prospect of having Renato as a PhD student at IMPA, and I would like to make it clear that {\bf I really, genuinely want Renato to do his PhD here.} His deeply rooted interest for mathematical analysis, his drive to work on analytical problems, his exemplary work both as a master's student and as a teaching assistant, and his collaborative and social spirit when doing mathematics align exceptionally well not only with my own perspective, but also with many of IMPA's values.  I am confident that, if given an opportunity, {\bf Renato will thrive in IMPA's PhD program, and will become an asset for my own research group.}
	
	Yours sincerely,
