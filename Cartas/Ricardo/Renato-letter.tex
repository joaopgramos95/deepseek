	\textbf{Letter of recommendation for Renato Andrade}
	
	
	To whom it may concern, 
	
	I am writing to recommend the candidate \textbf{Renato Andrade} in \textbf{very high terms} for a PhD position at IMPA.  \\ 
	
	I have been Renato's master's thesis advisor for around 5 months.  In our first interaction, it was already clear to me that Renato had a deep interest for mathematical analysis: indeed, he did his undergrad under the guidance of Dami\~ao Ara\'ujo at UFPB, where he developed a taste for analysis early on.  Within minutes of getting to know me, he already asked for a topic in which he could work in parallel to the courses at IMPA. 
	
	I believe this to be one of Renato's strongest suits: he is {\bf passionate about research in analysis}, to the point that he is {\bf always determined to learn more.} As such, I have only had {\bf exciting interactions} with Renato so far. We have been meeting about once every two weeks to discuss mathematics, and he has always asked the right question about the problems he works on. 
	
	Indeed,  he came to me a couple of months ago with the very simple question of explaining intrinsically Duhamel's formula.  After I explained to him its proof, he told me he had already managed to prove it - his issue was with the motivation behind it.  As it turns out, this was a question I had never encountered before, especially posed by a master's student. The ensuing discussion produced not only a satisfactory answer to his question, but also showed me a new way to think about the topic at hand. 
	
	Examples of such instances do not stop there. He is one of the most participative students in my current course (``PDE: Linear theory''), and he has several times asked fundamental questions about generalizations to theorems seen in class.  In more than one of such instances, his question directly led to topics which would later be seen in class, which showcases a deep understanding of the usefulness of the tools discussed.  Furthermore, he has been doing very well in the course itself, where he so far received an above-average grade in the first exam -- roughly equivalent to an A- --, and where he tries to solve and submit all the proposed exercises in the assignment,  regardless of their difficulty. 
	
	Speaking of grades, I {\bf strongly believe} Renato's grades do not fully depict how competent he actually is.  Indeed, Renato is so deeply interested in mathematical analysis that it seems to be the case that he has mostly worked on learning topics besides the ones from the courses he takes at IMPA, mainly because he is more interested in the other (more analytic) ones.  While I do believe that this is one of the points where Renato can improve the most, by simply better allocating and balancing his time and effort,  I believe there is a `glass-half-full' way to look at this: he has shown that he is capable of keeping mostly reasonable grades (most of them around B+) in spite of starting out with a tough semester at IMPA, while, at the same time, learning more about his topic of interest.  This makes me excited about what he might be able to achieve if given the freedom to focus mostly on analysis. 
	 
	We have, furthermore, recently started working together on a topic which shall eventually lead to his master's thesis and - given he gets admitted to IMPA's PhD program - should naturally progress to a first research topic in his PhD. Namely, we have started discussing the problem `A remark on harmonic gradients in dimensions $\geq 3$', by J. Bourgain and T. Wolff. In spite of the technical difficulty of that paper, I believe that the techniques Renato is currently learning at `PDE: linear theory', together with his natural drive to work on analytic problems, will result in a successful first research project. 
	
	Finally, another quality one cannot ignore about Renato are his {\bf social skills}. Since the first day we interacted, Renato has always been respectful, while also being easy-going and talkative. It is therefore clear to me that he enjoys the collaborative aspect of mathematics,  since he effectively engages in discussions about the problems in the assignments from the `PDE: Linear Theory' class with colleagues, while also keeping an independent mind whenever it comes to writing up the problems themselves. 
	
	These qualities definitely make me \textbf{very excited} to work with Renato in a possible PhD at IMPA. His deeply rooted interest for mathematical analysis, his drive to work on analytical problems, and his collaborative and social spirit when doing mathematics align exceptionally well not only with my own perspective, but also with many of IMPA's values.  I am confident that, if given an opportunity, {\bf Renato will thrive in IMPA's PhD program,  as well as he will become an asset for my own research group.} 
	
	Yours sincerely,
