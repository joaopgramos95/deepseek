## Known Invalid Step

The lemma named like `Green kernel gap`, asserting

```tex
P_zG(z,\zeta)
\le
C_{\alpha,s}\bigl(Q_{s,z}G(z,\zeta)-P_zG(z,\zeta)\bigr),
```

is false or at least not justified near `|\zeta| -> 1`.

Reason: the leading boundary asymptotic of `G(z,\zeta)` is proportional to the
Poisson kernel

```tex
(1-|\zeta|^2) (1-|z|^2)/|z-\xi|^2.
```

For radial averaging operators `P` and `Q_s`, the angular average of the
Poisson kernel is constant on centered circles. Hence the leading terms in
`P` and `Q_s` cancel. The gap is not first order in `1-|\zeta|`.

Do not reuse this lemma in its current form.

## Core Strategy To Implement

Replace the false first-order Green-gap argument by a decomposition of the
Riesz potential into two parts:

```tex
\psi = \psi_{\mathrm{int}} + \psi_{\mathrm{bdry}}.
```

The intended roles are:

1. **Interior poles**: poles with distance from the boundary bounded below.
   These should still be controlled by a genuine first-variation gap
   `P(\psi_int)-Q_s(\psi_int)`.

2. **Boundary-layer poles**: poles with `1-|\zeta|` small. On compact subsets
   containing the optimizing disk boundary, their Green kernels are almost
   harmonic Poisson profiles after normalization. They should be absorbed by
   the harmonic Schur-complement/free-boundary calculation rather than by the
   linear Green gap.

3. **Remainder**: the non-harmonic error in the boundary expansion should be
   lower order and paid for either by the small tail gap or by the same
   perturbative quadratic form.

## Main Lemma To Prove

Prove a lemma of the following form, with precise norms and constants.

```tex
\begin{numlemma}[Boundary-layer absorption]
Fix \alpha>-1 and s\in(0,\infty). There exist constants
\eta_{\alpha,s}, c_{\alpha,s}>0 with the following property.
Let
\[
\varphi=h+\psi,\qquad
\psi=-\int_{\D}G(\cdot,\zeta)\,d\mu(\zeta),\qquad \mu\ge0,
\]
be the Riesz decomposition of a real-analytic subharmonic function, normalized
by P(\varphi)=0. If the optimized bathtub deficit is sufficiently small, then
after splitting \mu into interior and boundary-layer pieces one has a lower
bound
\[
\mathcal D_s(\varphi)
\ge
c_{\alpha,s}\Big(
  \mathcal Q_s(h+h_{\mathrm{bdry}})
  + \operatorname{Gap}_{\mathrm{int}}(\psi_{\mathrm{int}})
  + \operatorname{Err}_{\mathrm{bdry}}
\Big),
\]
where h_{\mathrm{bdry}} is the harmonic Poisson part generated by the
boundary-layer poles, \mathcal Q_s is the Schur-complement quadratic form, and
the error terms control the difference between the true boundary-layer Green
potential and its harmonic model.
\end{numlemma}
```

This is schematic. The actual implementation may use a contradiction/compactness
version instead:

```tex
If g_j -> 1 in L^2, the optimized deficits tend to zero, and the harmonic
parts are centered, then the combined boundary-layer harmonic profile plus the
interior Green gap tends to zero in the norm needed to invoke the local
half-stability theorem.
```
